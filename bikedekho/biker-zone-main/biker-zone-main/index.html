What are the most important things I should know about riding a bike?
Safety First! Always obey the rules of the road. Obey all traffic signals, signs, and laws. Get in the mindset of “driving” your bike—not just “riding” your bike. This will help you be a more focused and legally compliant bike rider.
How can I tell if my helmet is old and I need a new one?
Check the manufacturer's guidelines for the lifespan of your helmet. If you notice any cracks, dents, or significant wear, it's time for a replacement. Additionally, if you've been in an accident, you should replace your helmet regardless of its age.
My bike has been in storage is it safe to ride?
Before riding a bike that has been in storage, it's important to check a few key things: Inspect the tires for proper inflation and any signs of damage. Check the brakes to ensure they are functioning properly. Lubricate the chain and gears if necessary. If you're unsure about the bike's condition, consider taking it to a professional for a tune-up.
What rules should I follow when riding my bike?
Always wear a helmet and follow traffic laws. Use hand signals to indicate turns and stops. Stay visible by wearing bright colors and using lights at night. Be aware of your surroundings and watch for pedestrians and other vehicles.
